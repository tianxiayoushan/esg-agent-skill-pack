# Evidence Status Policy

Every ESG claim must carry one evidence status.

| Status | Use when | Drafting rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verified | The user supplied source material or public report material and the wording follows that material. | Cite or describe the evidence location. Do not treat this as legal verification, audit, assurance, regulator approval, board approval, external publishability, completeness, or freedom from error. |
| Needs confirmation | The claim may be plausible but needs owner, legal, assurance, or source confirmation. | Keep wording conditional and assign an owner. |
| Missing data | Required evidence has not been supplied. | Ask for the missing source or metric. |
| Do not claim | The claim is unsupported, promotional, misleading, or outside the evidence. | Remove or replace with neutral wording. |

If a metric is externally assured, state the assurance level separately, such as reasonable assurance, limited assurance, or assurance status not provided. Do not equate `Verified` with audited or assured.

Do not upgrade a status without source evidence provided by the user. If evidence is partial, use `Needs confirmation` or `Missing data`.
