# Public Source Research Map

This research map supports realistic internal pilot scenario design for the ESG Skill Pack. It is not a legal checklist and does not determine regulatory compliance. Users must verify official source text and obtain professional review before external publication, filing, submission, disclosure, or ESG report inclusion.

## Design Implications For The Skill Pack

- Preserve evidence status on every material ESG claim.
- Add output-use classification: draft, internal working paper, board pre-read, external disclosure, regulatory filing, investor response.
- In listed-company or investor contexts, always flag selective disclosure, inside information, market-sensitive information, and approved-channel review.
- Treat Scope 1, Scope 2, Scope 3, supplier audits, ratings, targets, assurance, compliance, and customer impact as evidence-dependent.
- For low-AI-maturity prompts, ask minimal clarifying questions and propose a safe review path before drafting external wording.
- For bilingual prompts, preserve evidence status labels and risk flags even when drafting in English from Chinese instructions.
